notes: Robert Peck was driven from his parish in Hingham England because of his zealous Puritan views. He fled to Massachusetts in 1638 with 132 of his parisioners including his brother, Joseph (see Joseph Peck). He led his followers to found Hingham MA where he was ordained the first pastor of the church. In 1641, with the Puritans winning the English Civil War, Peck returnedto England and in 1646 resumed his pulpit in Hingham, Norfolk, England. His wife and son returned with him but his daughter, Anne,had married Capt. John Mason and she remained in America.
